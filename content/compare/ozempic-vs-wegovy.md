Ozempic and Wegovy contain the exact same active ingredient — semaglutide — made by the same company, Novo Nordisk. So why are there two different products, and does it matter which one you take? The differences are more significant than you might think.

## The Quick Answer

**Ozempic** is approved for type 2 diabetes management. The maximum dose is 2.0mg per week. Weight loss is a beneficial side effect, not its primary purpose.

**Wegovy** is approved specifically for weight loss (chronic weight management). The maximum dose is 2.4mg per week — 20% higher than Ozempic. It's also now available as an oral pill as of January 2026.

Same molecule. Different doses. Different approved uses. Different insurance coverage.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

**Active ingredient:** Both contain semaglutide, a GLP-1 receptor agonist.

**FDA approval:** Ozempic is approved for type 2 diabetes (2017). Wegovy is approved for chronic weight management in adults with obesity (BMI ≥30) or overweight (BMI ≥27) with at least one weight-related condition (2021). Wegovy is also approved for adolescents aged 12+.

**Maximum dose:** Ozempic tops out at 2.0mg weekly. Wegovy goes up to 2.4mg weekly. This higher dose is significant — clinical trials showed that the 2.4mg dose produced meaningfully more weight loss than 1.0mg.

**Weight loss results:** In clinical trials, Ozempic at its maximum dose showed approximately 10-15% body weight reduction. Wegovy at 2.4mg showed approximately 15-17% body weight reduction. The extra dose makes a real difference for many people.

**Administration:** Both are once-weekly subcutaneous injections using a pre-filled pen. Wegovy also launched an oral pill form in January 2026.

**Cardiovascular benefits:** Both have demonstrated cardiovascular benefits in clinical trials. Wegovy received a specific FDA indication for reducing cardiovascular risk in adults with obesity and established cardiovascular disease.

## The Cost Difference

This is where things get interesting and potentially confusing.

**Ozempic** has historically been slightly cheaper, with a list price around $900-$1,000 per month. Because it's approved for diabetes, it's generally more widely covered by insurance for patients with type 2 diabetes.

**Wegovy** has a list price of approximately $1,350 per month. Insurance coverage for weight loss medications has historically been more limited than for diabetes medications, though this has been improving significantly in 2025 and 2026.

**The generic factor:** With semaglutide's US patent expiring in March 2026, generic versions are entering the market. This will primarily affect pricing for semaglutide products across the board, though the exact timing and pricing of generics varies.

**Recent price changes:** Novo Nordisk has reduced prices for both products through direct-to-consumer programmes and government negotiations. Cash-pay options around $349/month are now available for some patients.

## Which One Should You Ask About?

**Choose Ozempic if:** You have type 2 diabetes and want the blood sugar management benefits with weight loss as a bonus. You may also have an easier time getting insurance coverage through the diabetes pathway.

**Choose Wegovy if:** Your primary goal is weight loss, you don't have type 2 diabetes, or you want access to the higher 2.4mg dose for maximum weight loss effect. If you prefer a pill over injections, Wegovy's new oral formulation is also an option.

**Important note:** Many doctors prescribe Ozempic off-label for weight loss because insurance is more likely to cover it when prescribed for diabetes. This is a conversation to have with your healthcare provider about the best approach for your specific situation and insurance coverage.

## Can You Switch Between Them?

Yes. Because both contain semaglutide, switching is generally straightforward. Your doctor can transition you from one to the other, typically matching the dose you were already taking. The most common switch is from Ozempic to Wegovy when someone who started the medication for diabetes decides they want the higher maximum dose for weight loss.

## The Bottom Line

Ozempic and Wegovy are essentially the same medication at different doses with different approved uses. The "best" choice depends on your diagnosis, your goals, your insurance coverage, and your doctor's recommendation. Neither is inherently better — they serve different clinical needs using the same proven molecule.
