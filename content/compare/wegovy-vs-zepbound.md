If your doctor has recommended a GLP-1 medication specifically for weight loss, you're likely choosing between two options: Wegovy (semaglutide) and Zepbound (tirzepatide). Both are FDA-approved for chronic weight management. Both produce significant, clinically meaningful weight loss. But they're not identical.

## The Key Difference

**Wegovy** contains semaglutide, a GLP-1 receptor agonist made by Novo Nordisk. It targets one hormone receptor system.

**Zepbound** contains tirzepatide, a dual GLP-1/GIP receptor agonist made by Eli Lilly. It targets two hormone receptor systems simultaneously.

## Weight Loss Results

Clinical trials consistently show tirzepatide (Zepbound) producing greater average weight loss than semaglutide (Wegovy), though both produce clinically significant results.

**Wegovy:** The STEP clinical trial programme showed average weight loss of approximately 15-17% of body weight over 68 weeks at the 2.4mg dose.

**Zepbound:** The SURMOUNT trial programme showed average weight loss of approximately 20-25% of body weight over 72 weeks at the highest dose (15mg).

That's a meaningful difference — roughly 5-8 percentage points more weight loss with Zepbound on average. For someone weighing 100kg, that could mean an additional 5-8kg of weight loss.

However, averages mask individual variation. Some people lose more weight on Wegovy than others do on Zepbound. Response is personal.

## Availability and Formulations

**Wegovy** is available as a once-weekly injection and, as of January 2026, an oral daily pill. This gives patients who are needle-averse a genuine alternative.

**Zepbound** is currently available only as a once-weekly injection. Oral tirzepatide is in development but not yet approved.

## Cost and Insurance

Both medications carry similar list prices in the $1,000-$1,350 per month range. However, there are important differences in access.

Wegovy has been on the market longer and generally has broader insurance formulary coverage. The expiry of the semaglutide patent in March 2026 is expected to bring generic options that could reduce costs significantly.

Zepbound launched more recently and is still building insurance coverage. Eli Lilly has been aggressive with direct-to-consumer pricing, offering vials at reduced prices through their LillyDirect programme.

## Side Effect Profile

Both medications share the standard GLP-1 gastrointestinal side effect profile. Nausea, constipation, diarrhoea, and vomiting are the most common with both. Studies suggest the rates are broadly comparable, though some patients report stronger gastrointestinal effects with tirzepatide, particularly at higher doses.

Both carry the same rare but serious risk warnings for pancreatitis, gallbladder problems, and the thyroid tumour warning from preclinical animal studies.

## Making Your Choice

**Consider Wegovy if:** You prefer having an oral pill option. Your insurance covers it more favourably. You want access to generic semaglutide for cost savings. You're concerned about starting with a less aggressive medication.

**Consider Zepbound if:** Maximum weight loss is your primary goal. You have significant insulin resistance. You've tried semaglutide and want something with a different mechanism. Your insurance covers tirzepatide products.

## The Bottom Line

Zepbound generally produces more weight loss than Wegovy based on clinical trial data. Wegovy offers more flexibility in how you take it (injection or pill) and may become more affordable as generic semaglutide enters the market. Both are excellent medications backed by robust clinical evidence. Your doctor can help you weigh these factors against your specific health profile and goals.
